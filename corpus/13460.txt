Description

Job Description:
Leidos is seeking a charismatic individual with the desire to shape the future of transportation. We're looking for a Data Scientist in McLean, Virginia that will help us discover the information hidden in vast amounts of spatial and non-spatial data, and help us make smarter decisions to deliver even better results. Your primary focus will be in applying data mining techniques, doing statistical analysis, and prepare technical reports. The successful candidate will have an ability to perform a variety of duties surrounding data and machine learning technology as well as quickly adapt to research and development (R&D) solutions.

Leidos operates FHWAs Saxton Transportation Operations Laboratory (STOL), where emerging technologies are developed to improve transportation safety, mobility, and environmental impacts throughout the nation and internationally. Saxton Lab provides a variety of services to support the advancement and deployment of vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V) / vehicle-to-infrastructure (V2I) technologies, cooperative driving automation (CDA) systems, modeling and simulation, open source software, data management, and cloud-based systems.

Were a hands-on lab where you not only get to see quick results from your work, but help develop the solutions in an agile and supportive environment. We develop hardware, software, and vehicle solutions from concept to design, development, prototyping, testing, integration, and deployment.

This position is located at FHWAs Turner-Fairbank Highway Research Center in McLean, VA, and may provide the opportunity to travel to meet with industry experts and partners, provide technical support, and participate or present at conferences.

Responsibilities:

•Mine and analyze raw data to drive optimization and improvement of R&D solutions.
•Assess the effectiveness and accuracy of new data sources and data gathering techniques.
•Develop custom data models and algorithms to apply to data sets.
•Implement new statistical or other mathematical methodologies as needed for specific models or analysis.
•Initiate the experimentation of various technologies and tools with the vision of creating innovative data driven insights for the business.
•Identify data patterns and trends.
•Perform graphical model analysis
•Possess a solid machine-learning foundation
•Establishing efficient, automated processes for model development, validation, implementation, and large-scale data analysis
•Prepare presentations and technical reports to illustrate and explain analyses, tools, applications and findings.

Required Education & Experience:

•A Bachelors degree in a relevant technical field with 8+ years experience or a Master's degree in a relevant technical field with 6+ years experience.
•Ability to obtain and maintain a Public Trust clearance.
•Must have valid drivers license and ability to pass DMV background check.
•Experience processing, cleansing, and verifying the integrity of data used for analysis.
•Proficiency with program languages, tools, and applications (e.g. Python, SQL, R, ArcGIS, Tableau, ggplot) for analysis and visualization of transportation data.
•Understanding of machine learning techniques and algorithms and AI.
•Experience with applied statistics skills, such as distributions, statistical testing, regression, etc.
•Ability to work independently and with small teams.
•Ability to work under pressure and meet tight deadlines across multiple projects simultaneously, prioritizing demands on a daily, weekly, and monthly basis.
•Excellent verbal and written communication skills, along with the ability to listen and accurately identify client needs and issues.

Preferred Experience:

•Experience with handling unstructured data.
•Proficiency in using query languages such as Hive, Pig.
•Ability to perform scripting and programming skills such as Java, C/C++ is a plus
•Experience with NoSQL databases, such as MongoDB, Cassandra, HBase.
•Working knowledge of ITS, V2V/V2I, and/or CDA systems.
•Experience and understanding of transportation standards (e.g., SAE J2735, IEEE 1609, NTCIP 1202, etc.).
