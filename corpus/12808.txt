Mid-level Environmental Scientist/Engineer – Tulsa, OK

As an industry leader, Trihydro provides U.S. engineering and environmental services dedicated to providing clients across many industries with effective, responsive, and safe solutions. Our company offers you a safe, professional, and positive working environment as we tackle some of the most complex environmental challenges.


Responsibilities
In your role as an Environmental Scientist duties will include soil and groundwater quality assessment and environmental sampling, operations and maintenance support for remediation systems, data review, and assisting with technical report preparation. Applicants must have excellent technical writing skills, a clean driving record, and a demonstrated commitment to safe operations. This position will require field work, such as supervising subcontractors and sample collection, and some travel (up to 50% of work time may include travel).


Location
The preferred candidate will be based in Tulsa, OK.


Required Qualifications:


5-10 years of relevant experience
A Bachelor’s degree or Master’s degree (MS preferred) in Environmental Science, Geology, Biology, Engineering, or a related field
Exceptional written and oral communication skills with the ability to communicate effectively with clients and project team members
Good employment or academic history with a commitment to safe work practices
Initiative and motivation, with an emphasis on completing project deliverables on schedule
Reliable, team-oriented work habits
Ability to travel to client sites and other Trihydro offices
A clean driving record
Preferred Qualifications:


Strong technical and problem-solving skills, including attention to detail and accuracy, as well as a commitment to producing high-quality deliverables
Strong mechanical aptitude
Direct industry employment history in environmental compliance, within an industrial setting, or a related field
Experience with and an interest in contributing to site assessment, subsurface investigations, evaluating fate and transport of contaminants, development of site conceptual models, and evaluation of remedial alternatives
