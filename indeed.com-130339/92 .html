We value diversity and strongly encourage applications from individuals from all identities and backgrounds, like yours. DATA SCIENTIST, Resource Development, to play a leading role in providing analysis to inform and advance MIT's fundraising mission. Will produce analytic models grounded in statistical disciplines to provide insight on organizational priorities, develop strategies to operationalize discoveries to improve upon business practices, and design creative ways to analyze and transform small and limited data to expand understanding of donors and MIT's fundraising effectiveness.  

Job Requirements

REQUIRED:  bachelor's degree, preferably in computer science, information technology, or similar quantitative discipline; seven years’ experience producing statistical models and analyses, including collecting, cleaning, evaluating, and synthesizing datasets using multivariate regression, logistical regression, and time series analysis; five years’ experience with statistical applications or programming languages, e.g., SAS, R, Python, Stata, etc.; ability to analyze and provide insight with respect to small or mid-sized data sets (consisting of observations ranging between hundreds to thousands, rather than millions, of rows), interpreting abstract questions, and providing concrete quantitative solutions; three years’ programming experience in SQL and Oracle or other DBMS; excellent problem-solving, interpersonal, and written and oral communication skills; attention to detail; and discretion with confidential information.  Must be able to produce effective, well-organized reports--that include data visualization--for the purpose of high-level decision-making; manage multiple projects/priorities and meet challenging and changing deadlines; and present complex statistical concepts/results to a variety of audiences.  PREFERRED: experience in higher education advancement services, particularly fundraising business processes, prospect management, and gift accounting within a large research university setting; interest in process documentation, investigative analysis, and process review with an eye toward efficiency; interest in learning new technologies; and comfort mapping business activities to IT systems/services.  Job #20902-10


This position will be hybrid, with a combination of on-campus and remote work.

Application material should include a resume and cover letter.
