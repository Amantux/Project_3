ESSENTIAL JOB FUNCTIONS

· Follows Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs), protocols and other instructions from management to perform release testing for raw materials and finished products, validate analytical methods, and qualify analytical instruments (OP/PQ/PV).

· Under supervision, the scientist will write/revise SOPs, test methods, protocols, and specification documents.

· Under supervision, the scientist will write and conduct out-of-specification (OOS) investigations and deviations.

· Conducts peer review of data generated in QC lab.

· Maintains the inventory of laboratory consumables and clean glassware.

· Performs routine maintenance of laboratory instrumentation including maintaining instrument qualification, coordinating and scheduling for maintenance / requalification services. Maintains complete and accurate records of instrument service and repairs in conformance with company SOPs and policies.

· Maintains general cleanliness of the lab, including personal bench space and common use areas.

· All other tasks and duties assigned as perceived and agreed upon by management.

KNOWLEDGE AND SKILLS

· Software proficiencies include, but not limited to Microsoft Word, Excel, PowerPoint, as well as data acquisition software.

· Analytical skills with hands-on instrumentation knowledge and experiences in HPLC, FT-IR, UV-Vis, Karl Fisher, pH, viscosity, particulate matter, dissolution analysis, etc.

· Has ability to work in a fast pace laboratory

· Good attitude, good work ethic, and great attention to details.

· Excellent written and verbal communication.

· Has some experiences with GLP, GMP, and GDP

Requirements:

· BS degree in Chemistry with 2 years of work-related experience, preferably in GLP/cGMP setting.

· MS in Chemistry with some related experience preferably in GLP/cGMP setting.
