2020-035-EGR: Mid-Career Signal Processing Engineer/Scientist

Directorate Engineering & Support Solutions
Location Beavercreek, OH
Riverside Research is seeking a full time or part-time Researcher to advance the state-of-the-art in the simulation of systems using data links and wireless networks. The systems may be connected using wire, RF, Laser, EO or other methods using the EM (electromagnetic) spectrum. You will be part of a cohesive 10+ member team of highly-skilled software and hardware researchers who love learning and strive to solve and transition critical technologies to the user. In this mid-career position, you will collaboratively advance your expertise with our growing team using all available information across all signal-processing domains.
Riverside Research is a flexible, family friendly, multidisciplinary not-for-profit conducting fundamental research in software implemented radios, collaborative systems, deep neural networks, statistical machine learning, plasma physics, nondestructive tamper and anti-tamper, system security, and biological systems.

All Riverside Research opportunities require U.S. citizenship.

Job Responsibilities:
This is a Research, Development, and Engineering position, you will
• Follow the data to achieve your objectives in the development, simulation, and testing of Global Positioning System (GPS) and other Global Navigation Satellite Systems (GNSS) waveforms
• Develop processes and code to emulate required signal processing components using Matlab, Octave, C, C++, Python, VHDL, and/or Verilog
• Develop alternative solutions to identified problems in 1-way and N-way data links
• Develop research plans, document your efforts, and propose new research thrusts
• Pull from all available sources to push the state-of-the-art

Qualifications:
• One year of demonstrated EM signal processing experience in VHDL or equivalent course work
• One year of demonstrated EM signal processing experience in C++ or equivalent course work
• Three or more years of relevant work experience
• Must support our Security Clearance process
• Bachelor’s Degree in a STEM related field or equivalent experience

Desired Qualifications:
These additional desired qualifications and/or skills for this position may set you apart from other well-qualified candidates.
• Strong presentation skills and demonstrated ability to interact with multiple customers
• Experience collecting and analyzing GPS and other GNSS waveforms
• Experience in data stream integrity analysis
• Experience with low-density parity-check (LDPC) and other error correction algorithms
• Experience in EM waveform analysis
• Master’s Degree in STEM related field
• Understanding of modulation, filters, atmospherics, and other artifacts of data links
• Demonstrated experience working as part of team or working alone
• Qualified for past or present access to TS/SCI material
